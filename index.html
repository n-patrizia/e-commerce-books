Hill of content website

Intriguing ways to browse an online bookstore

Overview

Hill of contents is a bookstore with stores in Melbourne and Sydeny. Its aims are to provide a wide range of books - published locally and abroad - in a pleasant browsing environment. The company prides itself on providing friendly and personalized customer service.

A redesign of their website was requested for a specific type of user: the spontaneous selector. The shopping patterns of this user are as follows:

  • “I loves chance discoveries.”
  • “Buying products because they are trendy or popular isn't a reason I buy them.”
  • “When something comes into my radar, I buy it.”

The design process

As part of my research for this project, I looked at Hill of Content Melbourne's website (they also have a Sydney store website) and their social media accounts with interest in their customers' comments. Thus, I was able to learn more about the product and tailor the site specifically for the spontaneous selector.

The product

I conducted a heuristic evaluation. In the 90s, Jakob Nielsen introduced a way to judge user interfaces based on ten well-proven human-computer interaction principles.

In the table abow, you can see that there were many system status errors, where mouse clicks and hovers yielded different outcomes. Lack of predictability creates a lack of trust in a brand and a product, and these errors must be corrected.

To see how competitors have addressed the issues of categorization, site navigation, I looked at Booktopia and Dymocks. I reviewed numerous checkout experiences from various fashion andgrocery retailers. In terms of impulse purchases, both of theseproducts are at the top of the list.

The user

I defined the following key words to clarify the Spontaneous Selector personality:

  • A casual attitude.
  • An instinctive response.
  • Is attracted to simple designs.
  • A tendency to act on impulse.
  • As a means of defining the user's perspective.

I conducted a user research study where I recruited candidates who agreed with the phrase: "when I shop, I love discovering things by chance." As they browsed the book section on booktopia, I discovered how much they love browsing.

As a reference, I sketched a user browsing diagram.

The solution

Users can now quickly access categories, best sellers, new releases, and more from a menu bar.
In order to avoid a complete change to the website at this point, I chose not to delete the browse by subject section on the left column.
I designed a quick and easy check-out process that doesn't require an account and I've fixed all heuristic errors.

My goal is to intrigue and appeal to the curiosity of the Spontaneous Selector through the browsing experience.
As a result, I added some tiles inviting the user to discover new titles while playing games like Quiz and Treasure Hunt.

Users testing

During the user testing with 5 users, there was no doubt that I was on the right track. One problem I found was the word "hunt", which made the majority of candidates think of pirates.
Among the candidates, one said:

"I expect random chance items. Stuff that the I won’t otherwise come across”, the word Treasure Hunt makes me think it’s about pirates”.

As a result, I changed the title of the section to "finding the unexpected" for the final prototype.
